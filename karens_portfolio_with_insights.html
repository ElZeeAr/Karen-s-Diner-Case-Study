Karen's Diner — Business Analytics Case Study

Course: Foundations of Business Analytics — Spring 2022 | Tools: Python, Excel, IBISWorld, Tableau
Forecast Outcome: Predicted closure due to unsustainable novelty model — validated in 2024–25.

Executive Summary

Analyzed Karen's Diner's business model using cost structure evaluation, consumer trends, and statistical methods. Findings indicated that high fixed costs, inflationary pressures, supply chain disruptions, and waning novelty would reduce profitability and sustainability.

Key Analyses and Insights

Key Insight

Rising input costs and inflation eroded margins in hospitality, making Karen’s Diner’s model more vulnerable.

Key Insight

Shift to takeaway/delivery eroded the novelty advantage of dine-in only models, reducing foot traffic over time.

Key Insight

Interest peaked in the early hype phase but quickly declined, reflecting the limits of novelty-driven growth.

Key Insight

Food quality had the strongest influence on repeat visits; high prices discouraged customers, while novelty added only short-term appeal.

Key Insight

Foot traffic showed a clear downward trend after initial hype, with forecasts predicting continued decline.

Key Insight

Customers were highly price-sensitive; rising menu prices directly reduced demand, limiting revenue growth potential.

Key Insight

Customer sentiment was polarized — while some enjoyed the concept, many complained about food quality, pricing, and poor service.

Key Insight

Search interest surged at launch but dropped sharply within two years, confirming novelty fatigue among consumers.

Key Learnings

  • Combined qualitative and quantitative data to forecast business outcomes.
  • Applied regression and forecasting to inform scenario planning.
  • Produced visual storytelling assets for stakeholder communication.
